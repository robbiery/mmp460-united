Being an immigrant student in this country is extremely stressful right now. We created this website to provide you with essential information for fighting unlawful deportation. Know your rights and share them with others!

5 Major Tips on What You Can Do as an Immigrant

The list below is based on the NYC government website. You can find out more at: https://www.nyc.gov/site/cchr/media/you-have-rights.page

  • If stopped by ICE agents on campus, you have the right to:

    Remain silent, speak with an attorney, ask for an interpreter [create ul]

  • Recording ICE agents may put you at risk

    Although, you are allowed to record as long as you do not interfere with the officers.

  • Find and Contact your Campus Immigrant Defense Office

    In New York, regardless of immigration status, you have the right to an education. All CUNY’s have Campus Immigrant Success Liaisons, and most other private schools in New York City have their own version of it.

  • Consult a trusted attorney.

    Immigrant New Yorkers who have questions and/or concerns can call 1-800-354-0365 for information and connections to legal assistance. Note: Public notaries who are NOT licensed attorneys cannot fill out immigration forms on your behalf, nor should they be giving you legal advice.

  • Create a Family Plan

    Discuss what you will do if you or a loved one is arrested. Organize your documents, memorize the phone number of an emergency contact, choose a standby guardian: a person you trust to take care of your child in the case you are detained [create ul]

About the editors

As New Yorkers, it can be hard to know who to trust, so we're here to share our stories with you.

  • My parents are immigrants, making me a first generation American. As an adolescent, my father made the choice to live in NYC despite barely knowing anything about American culture, much less the English language. He has spent the last three decades as a legal, permanent resident. That status is threatened everyday as the US government continues to harrass and prosecute immigrants without due process. He lives in fear of becoming a victim of unjust deportation. Even I am afraid, not just for my parents but for myself, being an outspoken citizen. This affects all of us, and that's why I wanted to create "United".

    Robbie B. CompanyName

  • I’m Pluto, and I am a first generation immigrant. My whole life, my family has lived in fear of persecution and deportation due to their own status. The thought of being ripped away from my family scared me while growing up, although I couldn’t imagine what the families going through deportation were (and still are) feeling. I believe that during times of division and fear, unity and community is what keeps hope alive. To the fearful—you are seen, your pain is real, and you belong here.

    Pluto C. CompanyName
